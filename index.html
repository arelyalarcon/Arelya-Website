My name is Arely Alarcon and I was raised in Los Angeles, CA and I have an interest in Political Science and the role of law within our society. When I was 17, I realized that the law treats people based on the constitution because at the time a case known as Masterpiece Cakeshop v. Colorado State Commision came along and brought many issues to the surface. One of the issues that was at stake was the rights of the LGBTQ community. I identify as a nonbinary person of color and that case opened my eyes as to why I want to study theories and where intersectionality meets the law. But more importantly what the law and the science of politics allows people mediation and an opportunity to seek opportunties that can benefit all of us and not just one specific type of identity. Poltical Science has always amazed me and given me an interest in how our government can function and also teach us from our mistakes.

I have particpated in a number of conferenced such as the QTPOCC (Queer Trans People of Color Conference) and Persist in order fro me to be more exposed to some of the issues that women, people of color, and LGBTQ community face. The conversations that are made allow us to create a safe environment where we can all talk about some of the issues that we see persist within our community and how society responds to it.
